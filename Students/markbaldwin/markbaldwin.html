Mark S Baldwin

Mark is a 3rd year Informatics PhD Student co-advised by Gillian Hayes (UCI) and Jennifer Mankoff (Carnegie Mellon University). His research interests include Human Computer Interaction and Ubiquitous Computing, particularly how they relate to Accessibility and Assistive Technology. Mark is currently exploring nonvisual forms of computer interaction that augment the traditional text-to-speech output model.

In addition to his primary research, Mark is actively works on a variety of DIY projects in Assistive Technology. If you are a student at UCI and interested in working with Mark on an existing project or have an idea of your own that you would like to pursue, feel free to reach out to him at baldwinm@uci.edu. You can learn more about Mark at his website.

Current Projects

  • Slow-refresh Tactile Graphics
